ENDANGERED BUTTERFLIES

Over the past 4 decades, more than 450 butterfly species have declined. at an average rate of nearly 2 percent a year.
The main reason for these butterflies being placed under the Endangered Species Act is mainly due to loss of habitat (agriculture, commercial, residential).
However, other reasons include: poor land management practices, excessive use of pesticides and herbicides, and loss of the butterfly's host plant that is necessary for it's growth and development.

The Western Monarch has plummeted in population by 99.9% but study revealed lesser known species, Bois duval's blue and california's state insect, the California dogface butterfly are heading towards extinction.
